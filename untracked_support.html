Frequently Asked Questions

How does Untracked detect workouts?

Untracked reads heart rate data from Apple Health to identify periods of elevated activity. You can review these periods and log them as workouts retroactively — even if you forgot to press "start."

What data does the app access?

Untracked reads heart rate samples and workout data from HealthKit. All processing happens entirely on your device. No data is sent to any server.

Why don't I see any heart rate data?

Make sure you've granted Untracked permission to read Health data. Go to Settings → Health → Data Access & Devices → Untracked and enable heart rate reading. You'll also need an Apple Watch or another device that records heart rate to Health.

Will retroactively logged workouts appear in Apple Health?

Yes. When you confirm a workout in Untracked, it's written back to HealthKit and will appear in the Fitness app and Health alongside your other workouts.

Does the app work without an Apple Watch?

Untracked needs heart rate data in Apple Health. If another device (like a Bluetooth chest strap) writes HR data to Health, the app will work with that data too.
